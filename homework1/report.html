Logistic Regression Model for Predicting CHD

We define a logistic regression model to predict the probability of coronary heart disease (CHD) based on all available risk factors. Formally, let i be the binary outcome for individual (i), where (Y_i = 1) if the individual has CHD, and (Y_i = 0) otherwise. Let (X{i1}, X_{i2}, , X_{ip}) denote the predictors (risk factors) for individual (i), including:

  • Sex (sex)
  • Age (age)
  • Education (education)
  • Smoking status (smoker)
  • Cigarettes per day (cpd)
  • History of stroke (stroke)
  • Hypertension (HTN)
  • Diabetes (diabetes)
  • Cholesterol level (chol)
  • Diastolic blood pressure (DBP)
  • Body mass index (BMI)
  • Heart rate (HR)

The logistic regression model is:
